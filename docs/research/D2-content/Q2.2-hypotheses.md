# D2.Q2.2 Hypotheses - Grammar Explanation Sample Quality

## H2.2.1 - Approved N1/N2 grammar explanations are not necessarily learner-ready

Prediction: sampled N1/N2 grammar explanations tagged `vi-editorial-approved` / `approved-by-user` will still score low on clarity because many contain placeholder phrasing like "sắc thái/cách dùng cần đối chiếu thêm." Effect size: at least 3 of 4 sampled N1/N2 items score clarity <= 2.

Falsifier: sampled approved N1/N2 items score clarity >= 4 and would not confuse a learner.

## H2.2.2 - Lower-level N3-N5 explanations are more fluent but uneven

Prediction: sampled N3-N5 explanations will read more naturally than N1/N2 imports, but short entries will still have pedagogic gaps. Effect size: N3-N5 average fluency >= 4, but at least 1 of 6 items still has clarity <= 2.

Falsifier: all N3-N5 sampled items score clarity >= 4 and no learner-confusion flags.

## H2.2.3 - Current status tags are weak predictors of quality

Prediction: status tags will not cleanly separate good from bad explanations. Effect size: at least one `approved-by-user` item is confusing, and at least one `manual-review-needed` item is usable enough not to confuse a learner.

Falsifier: all approved items are strong and all manual-review items are clearly poor.
