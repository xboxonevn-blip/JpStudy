# D2.Q2.2 Analysis - Grammar Explanation Sample Quality

## E2.2 Result

The sample confirms that approval metadata is not enough. All 4 sampled N1/N2 items are tagged approved, but all 4 scored clarity `2/5` and would confuse a learner because the Vietnamese explanation is mostly placeholder review text.

## Aggregate Scores

| Slice | Items | Fluency avg | Clarity avg | Accuracy avg | Confusion flags |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| All sampled | 10 | 3.3 | 2.9 | 3.3 | 5 / 10 |
| N1/N2 approved machine-origin | 4 | 2.0 | 2.0 | 2.0 | 4 / 4 |
| N3-N5 | 6 | 4.2 | 3.5 | 4.2 | 1 / 6 |

## Hypothesis Updates

- H2.2.1 supported. `4 / 4` approved N1/N2 samples scored clarity <= 2.
- H2.2.2 supported with caveat. N3-N5 average fluency was >= 4, but the N4 honorific sample scored clarity `2/5`.
- H2.2.3 supported. Approved status did not imply quality; `manual-review-needed` included both usable terse items and one confusing item.

## Red Team

This is only 10 items, and accuracy was judged against local English/source fields rather than external grammar references. It is enough to falsify "approval tag means learner-ready," but not enough to estimate exact corpus-wide defect rate.

## Action

1. Treat N1/N2 `approved-by-user` grammar explanations as suspect until sampled or rewritten.
2. Add a severity-aware grammar-quality sampler before doing bulk edits.
3. Fold `manual-review-needed` into D2.1 open-review counts; the scanner now does this.
4. Continue D2 with status-taxonomy/backfill before broad content rewriting.
