# D2.Q2.3 Hypotheses - Minna N3+ Coverage Gap

## H2.3.1 - Missing Minna N3+ is not a hard launch blocker

Prediction: Vietnamese N3+ learners commonly use non-Minna JLPT-specific series such as Shin Kanzen Master, Soumatome, Try, or Mimikara, so JpStudy can serve N3+ without a Minna-branded path if content quality is high. Effect size: at least 3 independent sources map Minna mainly to N5/N4 and non-Minna series to N3+.

Falsifier: Vietnamese market/course sources predominantly require Minna Chukyu as the standard N3+ continuation.

## H2.3.2 - Missing Minna Chukyu is still a product-positioning gap

Prediction: official publisher evidence shows Minna does have an intermediate continuation with Vietnamese support, so "Minna stops at N4" is false as a textbook-series claim even if true for this app's local assets. Effect size: official 3A pages show Intermediate I/II and Vietnamese translation/grammar notes.

Falsifier: Minna has no official intermediate/Vietnamese continuation.

## H2.3.3 - Route labeling matters more than brand completeness for beta

Prediction: the safest launch stance is JLPT-level route labeling, not "Minna path" continuity. Effect size: local assets already provide N3-N1 `ShinKanzen` and `hajimete` vocab routes, plus grammar/kanji by level, but not Minna Chukyu.

Falsifier: product UI or user expectations require a continuous Minna route from N5 through N1.
