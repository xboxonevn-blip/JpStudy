# D2.Q2.5 Analysis - Content Scope vs JLPT Rough Scope

## E2.5 Result

Local vocab volume is adequate on a cumulative rough-count basis, but kanji volume is not. N1 cumulative local vocab is `10,424 / 10,000` rough target, while cumulative local kanji is only `889 / 2,000`.

## Hypothesis Updates

- H2.5.1 supported. Cumulative vocab meets rough targets at every level: N5 `184%`, N4 `217%`, N3 `138%`, N2 `116%`, N1 `104%`.
- H2.5.2 supported. Cumulative kanji falls sharply at upper levels: N5 `159%`, N4 `99%`, N3 `75%`, N2 `69%`, N1 `44%`.
- H2.5.3 supported. Each level has hundreds of grammar examples: N1 `980`, N2 `764`, N3 `1000`, N4 `1000`, N5 `1180`.

## What This Means

- Vocab count is not enough evidence because Q2.1/Q2.2 showed machine-origin and quality gaps.
- Upper-level kanji scope is a real gap if the app claims full N1/N2 kanji coverage.
- Grammar example volume exists, but quality/link matching still matters.
- "Official JLPT scope" must be phrased carefully: current JLPT does not publish official item lists, so these are rough market/study-guide targets.

## Red Team

Rough counts are noisy and may be cumulative rather than per-level. The local kanji count may intentionally avoid duplicating lower-level kanji in upper-level lessons. The cumulative comparison reduces that issue, and still shows N2/N1 kanji under target.

## Decision

Do not claim full N1/N2 kanji scope. Vocab volume can be treated as quantitatively broad, pending quality review.

## Action

1. Prioritize D2.6 Han-Viet/Unihan spot check for upper kanji already present.
2. Create a future N2/N1 kanji expansion plan before launch claims.
3. Keep N1/N2 content route labels as "beta coverage" until kanji count and quality gaps are resolved.
