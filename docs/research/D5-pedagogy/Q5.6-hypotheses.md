# D5.Q5.6 Hypotheses - Cross-Skill Prerequisites

## H5.6.1 - Cumulative kanji coverage is still too thin

Prediction: adding lower-level kanji into the coverage graph improves vocab coverage, but fewer than 50% of kanji-bearing vocab entries at each JLPT level are fully covered by cumulative kanji assets.

Falsifier: cumulative coverage is >= 80% for N5-N3 or >= 60% for N2-N1.

## H5.6.2 - The app has scoped kanji practice, not prerequisite routing

Prediction: `KanjiPracticeArgs.kanjiIds` can open focused read/write sessions, but no product code maps a weak vocab item to "study these prerequisite kanji first."

Falsifier: a vocab/grammar mistake path builds missing kanji IDs and routes to scoped kanji practice before retry.

## H5.6.3 - Existing gates are soft foundations nudges

Prediction: foundations gating is a one-time soft suggestion based on broad kana/foundations completion, not item-level prerequisite enforcement.

Falsifier: vocab, grammar, or kanji routes block or sequence content based on per-item dependency state.

## H5.6.4 - Mistake remediation does not escalate to prerequisites

Prediction: repeated wrong answers increase mistake priority or ghost counts, but do not branch to prerequisite review, hint-first mode, or explanatory dependencies.

Falsifier: high `wrongCount` or ghost state launches prerequisite material before another quiz attempt.

## H5.6.5 - Grammar examples are linked, but grammar dependencies are not modeled

Prediction: grammar examples can support detail pages and practice, but grammar points do not declare required vocab, kanji, particles, or prior grammar points.

Falsifier: grammar content or DB schema contains durable prerequisite references consumed by practice/session routing.
