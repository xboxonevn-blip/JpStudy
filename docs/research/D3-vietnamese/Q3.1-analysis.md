# D3.Q3.1 Analysis - App Language Audit

## E3.1 Result

`app_language.dart` is structurally balanced but editorially inconsistent. The audit found `680` localized returns for each locale, `0` empty returns, and `0` TODO/draft returns. That means the main D3.Q3.1 risk is not missing switch cases; it is terminology quality and centralization.

## Hypothesis Updates

- H3.1.1 supported. English, Vietnamese, and Japanese all have `680` localized returns.
- H3.1.2 supported by terminology probe. Vietnamese copy mixes retained English/product terms such as `quiz`, `Kanji`, `SRS`, and `deck` with translated terms such as `thi thử`, `ôn`, `ôn tập`, and `phản hồi`.
- H3.1.3 supported. No blank/TODO/draft localized returns were found.

## What This Means

- Migrating to ARB is not needed just to fix missing keys.
- A terminology glossary would produce more immediate value than broad structural migration.
- `app_language.dart` can remain the central source short-term, but local helper copies outside it weaken that centralization.

## Red Team

Some retained English terms are product choices rather than errors. For example, `SRS`, `JLPT`, and `Kanji` may be acceptable for the target audience. The issue is inconsistent usage without a glossary, not the mere presence of English.

## Action

Create a D3 terminology table before editing UI copy. Candidate terms: `quiz`, `review`, `SRS`, `deck`, `kanji/Kanji/Hán tự`, `Hán Việt`, `thi thử`, `ôn tập`, `luyện`.
