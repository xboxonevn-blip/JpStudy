# D3.Q3.4 Analysis - Vietnamese Typography Quality

## Result

Fixed-seed audit found `2,196` Vietnamese string candidates and sampled `100`.

- Average score: `4.84/5`
- Clean `5/5` strings: `92/100`
- Issue count: `8` raw English term warnings
- Mojibake/question-mark warnings: `0`
- ASCII ellipsis/dash/spacing warnings: `0`
- Tone-variant warnings: `0`

## Hypothesis Updates

- H3.4.1 supported. `92/100` sampled strings scored `5/5`, above the `85/100` prediction.
- H3.4.2 not supported as stated. Raw English terms appeared in `8/100`, below the `10/100` prediction, but still high enough to require editorial cleanup.
- H3.4.3 supported. Tone-variant warnings were `0/100`.
- H3.4.4 supported. Punctuation/typography warnings were `0/100` under the current heuristic.
- H3.4.5 supported. All `8` warnings were outside `app_language.dart`, concentrated in feature-local hardcoded copy.

## Interpretation

The main D3 typography risk is no longer accent placement, mojibake, or punctuation. The visible problem is register consistency: Vietnamese UI often carries English product words such as `review`, `block`, `workspace`, `recipe`, `lane`, and `immersion`.

The clean score should not be overread. The sampler catches objective string-level warnings, not sentence fluency. D2 already showed that approved grammar explanations can still be pedagogically weak.

## Red Team

- The detector can undercount English terms because the whitelist/term list is conservative and does not flag all mixed-language phrases.
- The candidate set includes some domain data (`radical_item.dart`) and code-generated labels, not only user-facing UI.
- The sampler does not verify tone placement against a Vietnamese dictionary; it only catches a small list of common variants.
- A `5/5` score means "no heuristic warning", not "human-editor approved".

## Action

1. Treat Q3.4 as a triage gate, not final editorial approval.
2. Use the `8` raw-English sample hits to feed the D3 glossary cleanup queue.
3. Continue to Q3.5 ARB migration recommendation; the evidence still points to feature-copy modules before full ARB migration.

## Mental Model

Vietnamese typography in this app is less about missing accents and more about mixed product language. Once encoding is fixed, the eye stops tripping on broken characters, but learners still see a hybrid UI where `block`, `lane`, and `review` carry hidden product meaning. The fastest launch improvement is to standardize those high-frequency terms before attempting a full localization architecture migration.
